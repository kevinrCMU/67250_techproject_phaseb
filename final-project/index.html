Welcome to MonoMuse

MonoMuse is a contemporary museum exploring where art meets technology. Our rotating exhibitions, immersive installations, and hands-on workshops bring together artists, engineers, and curious minds under one roof.

Whether you're here for the first time or the fiftieth, there's always something new to see. We host three floors of exhibition space in Pittsburgh's Oakland neighborhood, featuring everything from generative art and sound installations to hands-on workshops on creative coding. MonoMuse was founded in 2018 by a group of Carnegie Mellon alumni who wanted to build a space where art and technology aren't treated as separate things. Since then we've welcomed over 200,000 visitors and partnered with artists, researchers, and schools from across the region.

Plan Your Visit

We're open Wednesday through Monday, 10 AM to 6 PM. Late hours on Fridays until 9 PM. Closed Tuesdays.

General admission is $18. Students and members get in at reduced rates. Kids under 5 are free.

Getting Here

MonoMuse is located at 4721 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA 15213, just steps from the Carnegie Mellon campus. Street parking is available along Forbes and in the East Campus Garage. The 61B, 61D, and 67 bus lines all stop within a block of the museum.

What's On

Our current exhibitions explore generative art, the history of human-computer interaction, and a new sound installation by local Pittsburgh artists. Stop by the front desk for a printed guide or check the exhibitions page for full details.
